What is the best puppy food?

A new puppy is a joy like no other. Maybe you are preparing to bring your new baby into your home, or maybe the pup is already there. And he’s hungry!

You have entered into a world of love and companionship, but there is a dizzying array of choices when it comes to what to feed your new friend.

Feeding him with the best puppy food is vital in these first weeks because your puppy is growing 20x faster than a young adult dog! It is in these first months that healthy patterns are established that will prevent future health problems like allergies, obesity, and joint problems.

With high quality puppy food, your little buddy will have everything he needs for a long healthy life.

Having too much or too little protein, vitamins, or minerals can cause serious problems in a fast-growing puppy, like joint and bone issues.

A puppy food with balanced nutrition will fuel your buddy for steady, healthy growth. What’s more, a bag of good puppy food will last much longer than a bag of the cheapest stuff. Quality dog foods have high nutrient density, so your pup won’t have to pig out to get all its protein. With this in mind, here’s what to look for in your little dog’s food bag.

The Ingredients We Want.

Whole animal products. The very first ingredient listed should be a simple whole animal product like chicken, lamb, or salmon. Your pup’s first nutritional priority is protein, and he’ll need a quality source of it.

Healthy grains. Your buddy does need his carbs, but lots of cheap carbs like corn or wheat are sometimes packed into a kibble to keep the price down. That’s nothing but a bunch of calories for your buddy. Look for grains like rice, maize, buckwheat, or oats at reasonable levels.

Natural fats and oils. Your puppy needs these for energy and overall health. A food with generous quantities of fats and oils from quality plant and animal sources will make his coat shine and his muscles strong.

Essential vitamins and minerals. These include vitamins A, C, E, B-complex, calcium, phosphorus, Omega 3 and Omega 6 fatty acids.

The Ingredients We Don’t Want.

Meat and vegetable by-products. This is a very vague term that can include old meat or products that came from sick or disabled animals. Yikes! Don’t leave this stuff to the imagination. Buy puppy food with recognizable meat ingredients.

Cheap grain fillers. Watch out for puppy food that is big on corn and wheat. While not inherently harmful, some brands include more of these cheap carbs than any other ingredient. Your puppy is a carnivore; pass on the corn.

Corn syrup. Much more harmful than grain fillers, this type of sugar is often used as a sweetener in dog foods. Too much sugar over time will cause overweight, diabetes and hyperactivity.

Top 4 best dry puppy foods for small breed puppies.

Small breed puppies (those who will mature at under 20 lbs) metabolize food very quickly. As a result, they must be fed more frequently than larger breeds. Feed your little tyke three or four times daily during her puppyhood. Insufficient calories during this stage of development can result in all kinds of unpleasantness like weakness, seizures, and growth impairment. Make sure it features at least 15% fat to fuel the rapid growth of these first months. For even smaller dogs, the miniatures and teacups, dehydration can be an issue. An easy fix is to mix a little bit of quality canned food (providing a high moisture content) into the normal dry kibble.
